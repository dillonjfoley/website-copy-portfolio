# Target Audience

## Primary Audience
The primary audience is made up of marketing managers, content teams, online educators, publishers, business owners, and course creators who want to turn written content into audio.

They may not have audio production skills, but they understand the value of making content easier to consume.

## Audience Profile

### Job Titles
- Marketing Manager
- Content Marketing Manager
- Communications Manager
- Course Creator
- Online Educator
- Publisher
- Business Owner
- Product Marketing Manager
- Training Manager
- Podcast Producer

### Company Type
- B2B SaaS companies
- Online education brands
- Publishers and media companies
- Small businesses
- Corporate training teams
- Agencies
- Coaches and consultants

## Pain Points

### Written Content Does Not Reach Everyone
Some customers, students, or readers do not have time to sit and read long content.

### Traditional Voiceover Takes Too Long
Hiring a voice actor, recording audio, and editing files can slow down content production.

### Audio Production Can Be Expensive
Professional voiceover work may cost too much for teams that publish content often.

### Teams Lack Audio Skills
Many content teams know how to write, but they do not know how to produce clean audio.

### Good Content Gets Used Only Once
A blog post, article, or guide may be published once and then forgotten instead of being turned into another format.

## Audience Motivations

### Reach More People
They want to make content easier to consume for people who prefer to listen.

### Save Time
They want audio content without managing a full production process.

### Repurpose Existing Content
They want to turn articles, scripts, guides, and lessons into another valuable format.

### Sound Professional
They want polished audio that reflects well on their brand.

### Improve Accessibility
They want to give their audience another way to engage with written material.

## Common Objections

### “Will it sound robotic?”
The copy should address quality and make it clear that the service uses natural-sounding voice options.

### “Will this fit our brand voice?”
The copy should explain that users can choose from different voice styles and tones.

### “Is this hard to set up?”
The copy should show a simple process: send text, choose voice direction, receive audio.

### “Why not just use a text-to-speech tool ourselves?”
The copy should position VocalForge as a service that handles voice selection, pacing, cleanup, formatting, and delivery.

### “Is AI voiceover good enough for professional content?”
The copy should focus on business-friendly use cases where speed, clarity, and consistency matter.

## Message Strategy

The copy should show that VocalForge is not replacing the value of human creativity. It helps teams get more mileage out of the content they already worked hard to create.

The main message should be:

> Your content is already written. VocalForge helps your audience listen to it.

## Desired Reader Reaction

After reading the landing page, the visitor should think:

> We already have content this could work for. This would be an easy way to turn our articles, training materials, or guides into audio.
