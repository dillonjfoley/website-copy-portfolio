# Target Audience: Nonprofit Website

## Primary Audience

The primary audience includes donors, volunteers, parents, teachers, school leaders, and community members who care about helping children succeed.

The website must speak to people who want to support the mission and families who may need the nonprofit’s services.

## Audience Profile

The ideal visitor may be:

- A local donor looking for a cause to support
- A parent seeking reading help for their child
- A teacher who knows students need extra support
- A volunteer who wants to serve children
- A business or church leader looking for a community partner
- A school administrator interested in literacy programs

## Pain Points

The audience struggles with:

- Not knowing how to help children who are falling behind
- Wanting to donate but needing to trust the organization first
- Feeling unsure whether their time or money will make a difference
- Parents feeling embarrassed that their child needs reading support
- Teachers having too many students and not enough one-on-one time
- Volunteers not knowing where to start
- Schools needing reliable community partners

## Motivations

The audience wants:

- Children to gain confidence in reading
- A simple way to make a local impact
- Proof that donations are used well
- Volunteer work that feels meaningful
- Better support for families and schools
- A stronger, more educated community
- Clear steps for getting involved

## Common Objections

Potential objections include:

- “How do I know my donation will help?”
- “Do I need teaching experience to volunteer?”
- “Is this program really free for families?”
- “Will my child feel embarrassed?”
- “How much time do I need to volunteer?”
- “What age groups do you serve?”
- “Can my school partner with this organization?”

## Messaging Strategy

The copy should be hopeful, warm, and action-focused.

The nonprofit should avoid making families feel ashamed or making the problem sound impossible to solve. Instead, the copy should show that reading support can change a child’s confidence, school experience, and future opportunities.

The message should make readers feel that every donation, book, tutoring session, and volunteer hour matters.
