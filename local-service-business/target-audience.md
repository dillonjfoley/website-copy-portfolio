# Target Audience: Local Home Cleaning Service

## Primary Audience

The primary audience is busy adults, homeowners, renters, and families who want a cleaner home but do not have the time or energy to keep up with it themselves.

They may need a one-time deep clean or ongoing cleaning support.

## Audience Profile

The ideal customer:

- Lives in the local service area
- Has a busy work or family schedule
- Feels stressed by household chores
- Wants a clean, comfortable home
- Values reliability and trust
- Prefers clear pricing and easy scheduling
- May have children, pets, guests, or a demanding job

## Pain Points

The audience struggles with:

- Not having enough time to clean
- Feeling embarrassed by mess or clutter
- Falling behind on bathrooms, kitchens, floors, and dusting
- Preparing for guests or events
- Moving in or out of a home
- Hiring cleaners who cancel, arrive late, or do rushed work
- Worrying about strangers in their home
- Not knowing what is included in a cleaning service

## Motivations

The audience wants:

- A cleaner home without giving up free time
- A service they can trust
- More time with family or personal priorities
- Help before guests arrive
- Less stress after work
- A fresh start after moving
- A home that feels peaceful and cared for

## Common Objections

Potential objections include:

- “Can I trust someone in my home?”
- “Will they do a thorough job?”
- “How much will it cost?”
- “What if I only need certain rooms cleaned?”
- “Do I need to provide supplies?”
- “Can I schedule around work or family?”
- “Is it worth paying for cleaning?”

## Messaging Strategy

The copy should focus on trust, relief, reliability, and local service.

The message should not make customers feel judged for having a messy home. Instead, it should make them feel understood and supported.

The tone should be friendly, calm, and professional.
